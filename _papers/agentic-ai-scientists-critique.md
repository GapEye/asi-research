This is an important counterweight to the most optimistic AI-science entries.
The authors argue that current agentic AI scientists work best as
co-scientists, not fully autonomous discoverers.

## Bottlenecks

The paper highlights problem selection, missing tacit procedural knowledge,
post-training pressure toward consensus, and benchmarks that lack feedback from
physical experiments.

## ASI relevance

Pro-ASI does not mean credulous. The fastest path to powerful scientific agents
is to understand where today's systems are structurally weak: real-world
failure knowledge, persistent world models, diverse hypotheses, and experiment
feedback.
